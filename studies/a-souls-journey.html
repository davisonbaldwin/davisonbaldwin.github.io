WORLD 02 · CHORAL DUOLOGY · TWO MOVEMENTS, ONE PASSAGE

A Soul's Journey

A one-man choir's passage in two movements: rest for the soul in an infinite landscape, then the yearning for sun under Franco's shadow. This page makes the same journey — scroll from the light into the dark.

PART I · REST MY SOUL
The design question

How do you funnel a complex system design into one working 3D virtual world — and make a one-man choir feel simple?

Figures fig. 2a — movement I, full performance “There is no score — every part was found by ear.”

A one-man choir is a complex system: many voices, one performer, no score, every part improvised by ear against the others. “Rest my soul” opens the duology with that system — and the design problem was to keep its complexity from reaching the listener. The answer was a single working 3D virtual world: one navigable landscape, built and animated to carry all the voices.

On screen, the system is visible and simple at the same time: a wall of my own faces, one for every voice, singing together — the choir made literal — while the world around them keeps opening. You can see exactly how the piece is made and still hear it as one thing. That is the funnel working.

Challenge

Present a system with many moving parts — a full choir sung by one person — without the machinery overwhelming the music. Complexity was the material; simplicity was the requirement.

Decisions

Funnel the entire system into one 3D virtual world. The landscape keeps opening as the harmony does — an increasingly infinite space that renders the choir as one continuous experience, not a stack of tracks.

Result

A one-man choir that reads as one world. Whether it lies past the edge of space or in the afterlife is left for the listener to decide.

My role

  • Composer & lyricist
  • Vocalist — all parts
  • Performer
  • Video editor & animator

Collaboration

  • Mixing & mastering — in collaboration with Felix Li
THE SOUL DESCENDS · THE SUN RISES
PART II · DAME SOL

Dame sol

Dame sol — “give me sun.” The longing for liberty as lived by the victims of Franco's regime: the yearning for freedom for the soul, and the hope of a better life.

The design question

How do you translate the longing for liberty into a distinct, open sound — while a complex 3D animation moves with it?

Figures fig. 2b — movement II, full performance “I wanted to own the final mix myself.”

The brief I set myself was translation — liberty had to become a sound, and the sound had to feel open and free rather than mournful. That character isn't only composed; it is engineered. The openness lives or dies in the mix, while the 3D animation is maintained in step with it, so the world on screen breathes with the sound.

Challenge

Translate a yearning for freedom into a distinct sonic character — open, unconfined — and hold a complex 3D animation in step with it.

Decisions

Work the engineering as part of the composition: first mixing in collaboration with Felix Li, then returning as assistant mix engineer and mastering engineer to remix and remaster the piece myself, protecting the open sound through the last mile.

Result

The close of the duology: a freedom-seeking sound carrying a specific history — in my own remix and remaster.

In the film, the ten voices face the sea. Each singer holds his own frame while open water and a low sun bleed through the portraits — every part of the choir looking toward the same light it is asking for. Dame sol: give me sun. The image and the title are the same sentence.

Taking the mix back mattered beyond the sound. Mixing and mastering had been the one stage of these works that passed through other hands. Owning it meant the decisions that create the open sound — the space, the air, how the voices sit against each other — were finally mine as well.
