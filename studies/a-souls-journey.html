CHAPTER 1 · CHORAL DUOLOGY · TWO MOVEMENTS, ONE PASSAGE

A Soul's Journey

A one-man choir's passage in two movements: rest for the soul in an infinite landscape, then the yearning for sun under Franco's shadow. This page makes the same journey: scroll from the light into the dark.

PART I · REST MY SOUL
The design question

How do you funnel a complex system design into one working 3D virtual world, and make a one-man choir feel simple?

Figures fig. 1a · movement I, full performance “There is no score. Every part was found by ear.” “When every voice is yours, the harmony can go anywhere.”

“Rest my soul” is a one-man choir: many voices, one performer, no score. Every part was found by ear, sung against the parts already recorded. The design problem was keeping that complexity from reaching the listener, so everything lives in one 3D virtual world: a single landscape, built and animated to carry all the voices.

The piece is really about the freedom that comes with singing every part yourself. There's no ensemble to write around, so the harmonic movement and the voice leading can go wherever I want them to. The piece explores that creative and harmonic freedom.

On screen you can see how it's made: a wall of my own faces, one for every voice, singing together while the world around them keeps opening. You see the whole system and still hear one piece of music.

Challenge

Present a full choir sung by one person without the machinery overwhelming the music.

Decisions

Put the whole system in one 3D virtual world. The landscape keeps opening as the harmony does, so the choir reads as one continuous experience instead of a stack of tracks.

Result

A one-man choir that reads as one world. Where that world is, past the edge of space or in the afterlife, is left to the listener.

My role

  • Composer & lyricist
  • Vocalist · all parts
  • Performer
  • Video editor & animator

Collaboration

  • Mixing & mastering · in collaboration with Felix Li
THE SOUL DESCENDS · THE SUN RISES
PART II · DAME SOL

Dame sol

Dame sol means “give me sun.” The longing for liberty as lived by the victims of Franco's regime: the yearning for freedom for the soul, and the hope of a better life.

The design question

How do you translate the longing for liberty into a distinct, open sound while a complex 3D animation moves with it?

Figures fig. 1b · movement II, full performance “I wanted to own the final mix myself.”

The goal was to turn the longing for liberty into a sound that stays open and free. That character lives or dies in the mix, so the engineering mattered as much as the writing. The 3D animation was built to move in step with it.

Challenge

Turn a yearning for freedom into a distinct sonic character, open and free, and keep a complex 3D animation in step with it.

Decisions

Treat the engineering as part of the composition: first mixing in collaboration with Felix Li, then coming back as assistant mix engineer and mastering engineer to remix and remaster the piece myself.

Result

The close of the duology, in my own remix and remaster.

In the film, the ten voices face the sea, with open water and a low sun showing through each singer's frame. The whole choir is looking at the sun it's asking for.
