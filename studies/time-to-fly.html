CHAPTER 3 · SONG + DANCE FILM

Time to Fly

The breakthrough of a man who realizes he can no longer let fear dictate his life.

Figures fig. 3a · dance film, full cut “Eight collaborators working toward one vision.”
The design question

How do you build out and incorporate the skill sets of many individuals into one professional product?

“Time to Fly” is about a man who decides to stop letting fear run his life. The project itself was really about product leadership. I wrote the song and played every instrument, building the record from scratch. Then I took it through each phase with the right people: engineering with mix engineer Felix Li, then choreography, dancers, videography, and costume design. One person can't make a dance film. Past the record, the project was leading a team of specialists, each with a craft I don't have, toward one product.

Challenge

Take one product end to end, from writing every note to a finished film, through skill sets I don't have: engineering, choreography, videography, costume.

Decisions

Build the core alone, then lead in phases: state the idea clearly and early (fear, then flight), cast each role deliberately, and give everyone room to solve their part.

Result

A professional dance film none of us could have made alone. Clear intent, stated early, let each expert do their best work.

Donathan Arnold choreographed the arc of the song, hesitation and then commitment, for two dancer leads, Kiara Lee and Robert Berry. Hannah Wyatt shot and edited the film, with Aviv Spitz on second camera, and Caifeng Hong's costume design gave it a visual identity I couldn't have come up with alone.

On set my job was artistic direction: keeping choreography, camera, and costume pointed at the same vision while each specialist worked in their own language.
