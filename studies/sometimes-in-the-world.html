CHAPTER 2 · BALLAD · POP + ORCHESTRA

Sometimes in the world

A pop-meets-orchestra ballad on the depths of loss and tragedy, and the importance of perspective.

Figures fig. 2a · video, full performance “Every instrument here is digital. That was the experiment.”
The design question

How do you hold a ballad's intimacy inside an orchestra's scale with one performer?

“Sometimes in the world” is about the depths of loss and tragedy, and the importance of perspective in carrying them. It's a pop ballad with an orchestra behind it, inspired by the harmony of Laufey. That pairing is the problem to solve: the bigger the arrangement gets, the harder it is to keep the song personal.

Challenge

Keep the listener close, one voice and one story, while the arrangement grows to orchestral size around it.

Decisions

Build the entire production in software: the strings from Cinematic Studio Strings, every other instrument digital, all of it performed and programmed by me.

Result

A full orchestral production made entirely with technology, mixed and mastered in collaboration with Felix Li, with a video I edited and animated.

This was my attempt at making a full production purely with technology. The strings are Cinematic Studio Strings, written part by part, and every other instrument is digital too. The question was whether one person with software could stand in for the room of players a ballad like this usually needs.

My role

  • Composer & lyricist
  • Vocalist
  • Pianist
  • All other instruments
  • Video editor & animator

Collaboration

  • Mixing & mastering · in collaboration with Felix Li
