CHAPTER 5 · SONG + ANIMATION

Life's Meaning

An investigation into the purpose of our existence, carried by an animated film.

Figures fig. 5a · animated film, full cut “We shaped the storyboard together before any animation started.”
The design question

How do you direct an animated world you didn't draw yourself?

“Life's Meaning” is an investigation into why we exist, in song. A question that big can't be illustrated literally without shrinking it, so the film became a collaboration with animator Caleb Worcester: we discussed the vision, he created a storyboard, and we edited it together from there. I performed every instrument in the arrangement myself: voice, piano, synthesizer, and the rest.

Challenge

Pair a philosophical question with images without reducing it to illustration, and realize a complex piece through a craft (animation) that isn't mine.

Decisions

Work through the storyboard: talk the vision out together, let Caleb draft it, then edit it together until the world matched the piece, and only then start animating.

Result

An animated film where the music and the image ask the same question, mixed and mastered by Felix Li.

The world we arrived at doesn't illustrate any lyric: an upright piano overgrown with ivy at the edge of a pond at dusk, a rowboat, willows, fireflies over dark water. It's a world that fits the question: something made by hands, going back to nature, still glowing.

Musically the piece is built the same way: voice and piano at the center, synthesizer widening the space around them. Every instrument is mine, so the build stays gradual and deliberate.

My role

  • Composer & lyricist
  • Vocalist
  • Pianist
  • Synth
  • All other instruments
  • Artistic director

Collaboration

  • Animation · Caleb Worcester
  • Mixing & mastering · Felix Li
